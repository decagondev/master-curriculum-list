Overview

Networking isn't limited to attending events or exchanging business cards. We'll explore the diverse forms of networking, from traditional in-person meetups to leveraging online platforms, social media channels, and so much more. Understanding these different avenues will help you diversify your networking approach.

Networking can be broken down into two categories: (1) In-Person Networking and (2) Online Networking.  Each form offers unique advantages and opportunities for you to connect, collaborate, and grow! Here is a further breakdown of networking within those two categories:

Traditional In-Person Networking:

  1. Industry Events and Conferences: Attending conferences, webinars, and seminars provides opportunities to meet professionals from diverse backgrounds and engage in face-to-face interactions.
  2. Professional Association Meetings: Joining industry-specific associations or clubs allows you to network with peers, share insights, and stay updated on industry trends and developments.
  3. Informal Meetups: Attending informal networking opportunities hosted by those in the community with similar interests and goals allows you to naturally build your network within the industry.

Online Networking:

  1. Social Media Platforms: Utilizing platforms such as LinkedIn and Twitter enables you to connect with colleagues, industry influencers, and potential employers, share updates, and participate in industry discussions.
  2. Professional Networking Sites: Websites like GitHub provide specialized platforms for you to showcase your work, collaborate on projects, and connect with like-minded individuals.
  3. Online Communities and Forums: Participating in online forums, discussion boards, and specialized communities (e.g., Stack Overflow for developers, Reddit communities, Slack communities, Discord groups, etc.) allows you to seek advice, share knowledge, and network with peers in their field.
  4. Informational Interviews (Coffee Chats): Conducting informational interviews with professionals in your desired career fields provides opportunities to gain insights, advice, and mentorship, while also expanding your network.
  5. Virtual Events and Webinars: Attending virtual conferences, webinars, and workshops via online platforms like Zoom or Webex provides opportunities to network with professionals from around the world without geographical constraints.
  6. Virtual Career Fairs: Participating in virtual career fairs hosted by universities, companies, or professional organizations allows you to connect with recruiters, explore job opportunities, and expand your professional network remotely.
  7. Alumni Networks: Leveraging the BloomTech alumni network and any other educational institutions you may have attended can lead to great warm leads when building your network.

By understanding and leveraging the different forms of networking, you can strategically expand your network, cultivate meaningful relationships, and unlock potential job opportunities.
