Overview

Cold outreach is outreach targeted at individuals you have no connection with. You can be strategic in who you target by finding people you have something in common with or whose career paths you are interested in. This can help make the emails more inviting to the recipient. For example, you can target graduates of other boot camps! While they aren’t in your network you already have something in common, a non-traditional path into the tech industry.

Like warm outreach writing this type of outreach can take time because you do want to personalize it to the specific person and your specific situation. To get started let’s practice together. 

 

Step 1: Create a list of 5-10 companies that you would be interested in working for and that you’ve seen hire junior tech talent. Be sure to avoid any companies that are currently on a hiring freeze and for the sake of this exercise any FAANG companies. Next, use LinkedIn to find someone who is currently in a role you would be interested in i.e. if you are targeting Frontend Developer roles find someone in a Frontend position with the company. Spend some time looking through profiles to find someone who either

  1.  Has a background similar to yours OR
  2.  Has a background you find interesting and would like to learn more about

Check out this Loom on how to search for someone on LinkedIn with a similar background. 

Step 2: Write your outreach message. Below are a couple of templates to help you get started. Please keep in mind these are examples and you should rewrite to fit your specific situation.

 

Email for someone with a background similar to yours:


Subject: Connect?

NAME – Hello!

I’ve been following COMPANY NAME  for a while now and came across your LinkedIn profile when I was doing some research. I noticed you came from a non-traditional tech background. I’m currently in the [INSERT PROGRAM] program at BloomTech and am interested in hearing a bit about your journey into the tech industry.

Right now I’m just looking for advice on how to stand out in the market and how to best utilize my bootcamp certificate and experience. Would love to ask you some questions about your experience at COMPANY NAME and how you set yourself apart in your job search.

Would you be willing to connect for a 20-minute call so I could ask you a few questions? Please feel free to grab a time that works for you on my Calendly: INSERT LINK.

I look forward to speaking with you soon!

NAME


Email for someone with a background you’re interested in:

Subject: Connect?

NAME – Hello!

I’ve been following COMPANY NAME  for a while now and came across your LinkedIn profile when I was doing some research. I was particularly interested in your contributions to [insert project or achievement].

I am currently in BloomTech’s [INSERT PROGRAM] program and would love to ask you some questions about your experience/background and pick your brain on the tech industry.

Would you be willing to connect for a 20-minute call? Please feel free to grab a time that works for you on my Calendly: INSERT LINK.

I look forward to speaking with you soon!

NAME

Tip: If you don’t already have a Calendly it can make scheduling calls like these very simple. Here's how to create a free account:

  • Set up your Calendly account here: https://calendly.com/app/signup

  • Link to your calendar and set appropriate availability based on their schedule. Here’s a Loom to help you get started!

Step 3: Find your contact's email address. You can find their emails by using free email finders such as Clearbit, Hunter.io, and Skrapp.io.
